To ensure the custom MSP430FR6043 PCB communicates correctly with both the sensors and the host PC, the following primary connections are established:

Ultrasonic Transducers: The upstream and downstream Jiakang 200kHz air transducers are connected directly to the analog front-end USS channels (equivalent to the X1 and X2 pins on the evaluation module).
Clocking Infrastructure: A high-precision external crystal oscillator is connected to the MCU's designated oscillator pins, flanked by two 33 pF load capacitors to ground.
Data Telemetry (UART): The UART TX, RX, and GND pins are routed to a USB-to-Serial bridge. This pipeline continuously transmits the calculated Absolute Time of Flight (AbsToF for UPS and DNS), Delta ToF (DToF), and Volume Flow Rate (VFR) to the custom Python GUI.
Programming Interface: The ezFET/JTAG pins are exposed to allow seamless firmware flashing using the TI Uniflash tool.


Hardware Configuration & Wiring

To program and communicate with the custom board, I am using the ezFET section of the EVM430-FR6043. I have removed jumpers J1, J2, and J3 to isolate the EVM MCU and established the following connections between the EVM ezFET headers and my custom board:

Comms: EVM (RXD/TXD on J3) → Custom Board (TP18 RXD / TP19 TXD)

I2C: EVM (SCL/SDA on J1) → Custom Board (SCL/SDA on J1)

Control: EVM (RST/TEST on J3) → Custom Board (TP1 Reset / TP20 Test)

Interrupt: EVM (IRQ on J1) → Custom Board (IRQ on J1)

Power: EVM (VCC/GND) → Custom Board (TP21 VCC / Pin 6 GND)
